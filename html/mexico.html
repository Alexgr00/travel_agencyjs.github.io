Mexico

Its incredible natural landscapes, jungles, cenotes and its beaches have not gone unnoticed either. Riviera Maya and Cancun are currently the towns where the country's tourism is concentrated, amazed by its incredible turquoise waters and the marine biodiversity of the Caribbean Sea. At the top of their coasts, the Mayans left them inherited precious treasures such as Tulum, Uxmal or Chichen Itzá. The Aztecs, for their part, left their capital, present-day Mexico City, the largest city in the country and one of the most frenetic.

Mexico

Los Cabos

Los Cabos is two opposite cities in one. Divide your stay between the city of Cabo San Lucas, which is full of nightlife, water sports, natural beauty, and adventure, and San José del Cabo, with its picturesque Mexican-town atmosphere, art galleries, and gourmet restaurants.

Mexico City

Mexico City, once the majestic Tenochtitlán, capital of the Mexica empire and later dubbed the “City of Palaces,” is now a vibrant and cosmopolitan metropolis that offers countless cultural, active, and even natural experiences to satisfy everyone. that traveler who wants to have a total immersion in the place they visit and is interested in becoming a local for a few days. In Mexico City one can touch history.

Tulum

The alternative-tourism center of Mexico's Caribbean coast. The beach is beautiful, the reef is close by, and there are no high-rise hotels or mega-resorts as in Cancún or the Riviera Maya. The Maya ruins of Tulum are the northern boundary of this region. Hotel eco-complexes range from basic thatched roofs to fancy air-conditioned rooms.
