Argentina

Buenos Aires

Think Buenos Aires and the European associations are inevitable. Its reputation as the "Paris of South America" has its roots in the late 1800s when the ideal of modernity among well-heeled Porteños (as Buenos Aires' citizens are known) was modelled on France.

Bariloche

Nestled in the middle of the Nahuel Huapi National Park, surrounded by exuberant mountains, ancient forests and crystalline waters, San Carlos de Bariloche capital of the province of Río Negro (Argentina) is an Eden for nature lovers.

Mendoza

The hustle and bustle of downtown Mendoza, Argentina, is contagious: Snack on empanadas at Mercado Central or take a jog in the Parque General San Martín. With its plazas and cafés, Mendoza is full of life and charm. But to capture the essence of the region, head to the outlying vineyards, where guesthouses (“bodegas”) welcome oenophiles with a glass of Malbec and a comfortable bed.
