Ney York

With more than 8.5 million people packed into some 300 square miles, it's no wonder New York City never sleeps. And if it doesn't, why should you? After all, there's always something to explore, from family-friendly attractions like the Metropolitan Museum of Art and Statue of Liberty to groundbreaking urban-redevelopment projects like the High Line and Brooklyn Bridge Park.

Las Vegas

The casinos! The shows! The parties! The pools! The food! The hiking? Despite all preconceptions to the contrary, Vegas is much more than the Strip, which is enough for most people. But venture off the always-lit path, and there's plenty more. Just a few miles in either direction yields family-friendly activities, unusual museums, impressive art, and breathtaking nature. Yes, Vegas.

San Francisco

Awash with sun and sea, parks and beaches, history and Karl the Fog (yes, locals have named their most famous weather pattern), this seven-by-seven-mile city flaunts its natural beauty proudly while inviting visitors and locals to dive deeper into its neighborhoods of hilly streets stacked with pastel-hued Victorian houses, Michelin-star and mom-and-pop restaurants, art and music.
