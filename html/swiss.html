Swiss

From the soaring Alps to sparkling lakes, Switzerland beckons with its breathtaking scenery. Journey aboard three of Switzerland's most iconic railways en route to the Italian Lake district, set out on an active hiking adventure amid glacier-clad mountains, or select your companions for a private expedition. Whichever trip you choose, you'll get immersed in culture, cuisine, and spectacular landscapes, and experience them from an insider's perspective.

Swiss

Zurich

Whether or not you have a Swiss bank account, this leader of the financial world has a lot more going on than just banks and high-end watch shops. Zürich is home to Roman ruins, religious history, natural beauty, and of course their famous chocolate. Take in the breathtaking views of Lake Zürich, go shopping on the Bahnhofstrasse, and have lunch at a centuries-old beer hall all in the same day.

Geneva

Geneva is an instant heart-stealer: a chic, worldly, French-speaking city that sits on the shores of its namesake lake and peers up to Europe's highest mountain, 15,780ft Mont Blanc. Such views whet the appetite for outdoor pursuits, which can easily be combined with a spin of the city's trophy sights.

Zermatt

With a dark hot chocolate in hand, I step out of the Petit Royal Cafe onto Bahnhofstrasse in the Swiss mountain village of Zermatt and make the two-minute walk to the railway station, where a sleek red-and-white train is waiting on the platform.
